## 2. Redesign Positioning

### Recommended Positioning

Kennedy Painting Pros should be positioned as the reliable local painting crew for homeowners and business owners who want a clean, professional finish without delays, mess, or uncertainty.

Professional painting in New Haven with clear estimates, insured crews, clean work, and communication from start to finish.

**Homeowners**

- Want the home to look fresh without managing the project themselves.
- Worry about mess, unreliable contractors, cost surprises, and poor finish quality.

**Business owners/property managers**

- Want the property to look professional.
- Need minimal disruption to operations.
- Care about timelines, insurance, and reliable communication.

## 5. CRO Recommendations

### Quick Wins

- Make the phone number persistent in the header on desktop and mobile.
- Use one primary CTA across the page: “Get a Free Painting Estimate.”
- Move the quote form higher on the page or add a short above-the-fold form.
- Turn long paragraphs into cards, bullets, and short sections.
- Add trust badges near the hero CTA: insured, free estimate, local service.
- Add service-specific CTAs under each card.

### High-Impact Changes

- Create dedicated landing pages for each high-intent service: interior painting, exterior painting, cabinet painting, commercial painting.
- Add a gallery section with before/after project images.
- Add testimonials with service type and location to make proof more believable.
- Add a stronger quote flow with project type, location, and timeline.
- Add FAQ content to answer price, timeline, prep, paint type, cleanup, and insurance questions.

### Test Ideas

- Test “Get a Free Painting Estimate” vs. “Schedule a Free Quote.”
- Test phone-first hero layout vs. form-first hero layout.
- Test testimonial cards directly under the hero vs. below services.
- Test service-area specific pages for neighborhoods with localized headlines.

## 7. Design Direction

### Visual Tone

Clean, local, trustworthy, and practical. The design should feel more like a professional home-service company than a generic contractor template.

### Recommended Visual Elements

- Large hero photo showing a clean interior or exterior paint finish.
- Before/after project gallery.
- Service icons or image cards.
- Review cards with customer names and locations.
- Sticky mobile call button.
- Clear quote form block with strong contrast.

### Avoid

- Long walls of text.
- Generic stock photos that do not look local or relevant.
- Weak CTA labels like “Submit” or “Learn More.”
- Hiding the quote form too far down the page.

## 8. Client Questions Before Final Design

- Which service produces the highest profit: interior, exterior, cabinets, deck, residential, or commercial?
- Which service should the homepage prioritize?
- Do they have real project photos or before/after images?
- Do they have Google reviews, Yelp reviews, or other review counts?
- What locations bring the best jobs?
- Do they want more phone calls, form submissions, or both?
- What is the typical project size and average job value?
- Are there seasonal services or offers they want to promote?
